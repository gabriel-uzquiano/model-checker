Examples

  • Ax Ay Pxy — Every x stands in P to every y
  • Ex(Px & Qx) — Something is both P and Q
  • ~ExPx — Nothing is P
  • Ax Ay (Rxy -> Ryx) — R is symmetric
  • ∀x∃y Rxy — Everything bears R to something
  • Pa & Qb — a is P and b is Q
  • a=b -> (Pa -> Pb) — Leibniz law instance
  • Ex(Px & Ay(Py -> y=x)) — There is exactly one P

Language

Constants: a, b, c, d, e  |  Variables: x, y, z  |  Predicates: P, Q, R, S, T

Atomic formulas use juxtaposition: Pxy, Rxa, Qab — no parentheses or commas between arguments.
